About this site

ni’s memo: my personal notes site—ideas I care about, reorganized so I can revisit them later. I write it for myself first, but you are welcome to read along if something here is useful for your own learning. The pages here are not official course materials; they are my own synthesis from the statistics curriculum at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I graduated.

UIUC statistics coursework

The topics on this site draw on and connect material I encountered across the following courses (listed by catalog number):

  • STAT 400
  • STAT 410
  • STAT 425
  • STAT 426
  • STAT 429
  • STAT 431
  • CS 481

How I use Nemo

I use these pages to keep definitions, intuitions, and recurring themes in one place: probability as a language, inference as disciplined uncertainty, and models as explicit assumptions plus methods that respect the data I actually have.
