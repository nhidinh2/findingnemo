Time series

When observations arrive in order, their dependence becomes a feature rather than a nuisance. Time-series models describe that dependence explicitly so we can estimate, diagnose, and forecast.

Stationarity

A time series $\{X_t\}$ is stationary when the mean $\mu_t$ is constant and the autocovariance $\gamma(s, t)$ depends on $s, t$ only through $|s - t|$. Stationarity is what lets us estimate covariances from a single realization.

AR, MA, and ARMA

AR(p): $$X_t = \phi_1 X_{t-1} + \phi_2 X_{t-2} + \cdots + \phi_p X_{t-p} + W_t, \qquad W_t \sim \operatorname{wn}(0, \sigma_w^2).$$ With the backshift operator this becomes $\phi(B)\, X_t = W_t$ where $\phi(B) = 1 - \phi_1 B - \cdots - \phi_p B^p$. Causality requires all roots of $\phi(B)$ to lie outside the unit circle, giving $$X_t = \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \psi_j W_{t-j}, \qquad \psi_0 = 1,\ \psi_j = \phi_1 \psi_{j-1} + \cdots + \phi_p \psi_{j-p}.$$ For AR(1): $$\gamma(h) = \frac{\phi^{\,h} \sigma_w^2}{1 - \phi^2}, \qquad \rho(h) = \phi^{\,h}.$$

MA(q): $$X_t = W_t + \theta_1 W_{t-1} + \cdots + \theta_q W_{t-q} = \theta(B)\, W_t.$$ Invertibility (all roots of $\theta(B)$ outside the unit circle) gives $W_t = \pi(B)\, X_t$ with $$\pi_0 = 1, \qquad \pi_j = -\bigl[\theta_1 \pi_{j-1} + \cdots + \theta_q \pi_{j-q}\bigr].$$

ARMA(p, q): $\phi(B)\, X_t = \theta(B)\, W_t$, with the parameter-redundancy rule that $\phi(B)$ and $\theta(B)$ share no common factors. The $\psi$ and $\pi$ weights satisfy $$\psi_j = \theta_j + \phi_1 \psi_{j-1} + \cdots + \phi_p \psi_{j-p},$$ $$\pi_j = -\phi_j - \bigl[\theta_1 \pi_{j-1} + \cdots + \theta_q \pi_{j-q}\bigr].$$

ACF and PACF

For a causal ARMA(p, q), $$\gamma(h) = \sum_{j=1}^{p} \phi_j\, \gamma(h - j) \quad \text{for } h > q,$$ with the MA cross term $\sigma_w^2 \sum_{j=h}^{q} \theta_j \psi_{j-h}$ added when $0 \le h \le q$. If $X_t = \sum_{j \ge 0} \psi_j W_{t-j}$ then $$\gamma(h) = \sigma_w^2 \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \psi_j\, \psi_{j+h}, \qquad \rho(h) = \frac{\sum_{j \ge 0} \psi_j \psi_{j+h}}{\sum_{j \ge 0} \psi_j^2}.$$

The partial autocorrelation $\phi_{hh}$ is the correlation between $X_{t+h}$ and $X_t$ after removing the linear effect of the intermediate lags. For AR(p) the PACF cuts off after lag $p$; for MA(q) the ACF cuts off after lag $q$.

  • AR(p): ACF tails off, PACF cuts off at $p$
  • MA(q): ACF cuts off at $q$, PACF tails off
  • ARMA(p, q): both tail off
  • Large-sample bands: $\hat{\rho}(h) \sim N(0, 1/n)$ beyond the cutoff

Estimation

Method of moments (Yule–Walker for AR(p)): $$\hat{R}_p\, \hat{\phi} = \hat{\rho}_p, \qquad \hat{\phi} = \hat{R}_p^{-1} \hat{\rho}_p, \qquad \hat{\sigma}_w^2 = \hat{\gamma}(0)\bigl[\, 1 - \hat{\rho}_p^{\!\top} \hat{\phi}\, \bigr].$$ For MA(1), $\hat{\rho}(1) = \hat{\theta} / (1 + \hat{\theta}^2)$ has a real root only when $|\hat{\rho}(1)| < 1/2$.

Conditional least squares / MLE: minimize $S_c = \sum_{t=p+1}^{n} W_t(\phi)^2$. For AR(p) the CLS estimator reduces to OLS, $$\hat{\phi} = (X'X)^{-1} X'y, \qquad \hat{\sigma}^2 = \frac{1}{n - p}\sum_{t=p+1}^{n} W_t(\hat{\phi})^2.$$ For MA and ARMA the innovations are nonlinear in the parameters, so Gauss–Newton is used: $$\hat{\theta}_{\ell+1} = \hat{\theta}_{\ell} + \Bigl[\sum_t \dot{W}_t \dot{W}_t^{\!\top}\Bigr]^{-1} \Bigl[\sum_t \dot{W}_t\, W_t\Bigr], \qquad \dot{W}_t = -\frac{\partial W_t}{\partial \theta}.$$

ARIMA and SARIMA

ARIMA(p, d, q): $$\phi(B)\,(1 - B)^d X_t = \alpha + \theta(B)\, W_t, \qquad \alpha = \mu\,(1 - \phi_1 - \cdots - \phi_p).$$ Differencing $d$ times turns a trending series into a stationary one.

SARIMA$(p, d, q) \times (P, D, Q)_s$: $$\Phi(B^s)\, \phi(B)\, (1 - B)^d (1 - B^s)^D X_t = \mu + \Theta(B^s)\, \theta(B)\, W_t,$$ adding a seasonal layer at period $s$.

  • Increase $d$ until the ACF stops decaying slowly; increase $D$ until seasonal spikes disappear.
  • Read non-seasonal $(p, q)$ from lags $1, \ldots, s-1$; seasonal $(P, Q)$ from lags $s, 2s, \ldots$
  • Model selection: lowest AIC/BIC, no significant residual-ACF spikes, Ljung–Box $p > 0.05$, roughly normal Q–Q plot.

Forecasting

The minimum-MSE forecast is the conditional expectation $$\hat{X}_{n+m \mid n} = E[X_{n+m} \mid X_n, X_{n-1}, \ldots].$$ For AR(1), $$\hat{X}_{n+m \mid n} = \phi^{m} X_n, \qquad P^{\,n}_{n+m} = \bigl(1 + \phi^2 + \cdots + \phi^{\,2(m-1)}\bigr)\sigma_w^2.$$

The best linear predictor on the last $p$ observations solves the Toeplitz system $\Gamma_p\, \phi^{(m)} = \gamma_m$, giving $$\hat{X}_{n+m \mid n} = \sum_{j=1}^{p} \phi^{(m)}_{nj}\, X_{n+1-j}, \qquad \operatorname{MSPE}(m) = \gamma(0) - \phi^{(m)\top} \gamma_m.$$ For invertible models the infinite-past forecast $\tilde{X}_{n+1} = -\sum_{j \ge 1} \pi_j X_{n+1-j}$ has error $W_{n+1}$ and MSPE $\sigma_w^2$.
